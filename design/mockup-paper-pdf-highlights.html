Boltzmann-based Exploration for Robust Decentralized Multi-Agent MCTS
(Extended Version)

Nhat D. A. Nguyen1,2, Duong D. Nguyen1, Gianluca Rizzo3, Hung X. Nguyen1
1Adelaide University   2University of Foggia   3HES-SO Valais
Abstract. Decentralized Monte Carlo Tree Search (Dec-MCTS) is widely used for cooperative multi-agent planning but struggles in sparse or skewed reward environments. We introduce Coordinated Boltzmann Monte Carlo Tree Search (CB-MCTS), which replaces the deterministic UCT with a stochastic Boltzmann policy and incorporates a decaying entropy-based bonus for sustained yet focused exploration. While Boltzmann exploration has been studied in single-agent MCTS, applying it in multi-agent systems poses unique challenges. CB-MCTS is the first to address this. We analyze CB-MCTS in the simple-regret setting and show in simulations that it outperforms Dec-MCTS in deceptive scenarios and remains competitive on standard benchmarks, providing a robust solution for multi-agent planning.

1. Introduction

Decentralized Monte Carlo Tree Search (Dec-MCTS) is an increasingly popular paradigm for cooperative multi-agent planning. Its anytime performance, domain-agnostic design, and online replanning capabilities make it well-suited for applications requiring scalability, fast response, and coordination across distributed agents — such as information gathering, precision farming, and networked robotics.

Current Dec-MCTS algorithms rely on the Upper Confidence Bound applied to Trees (UCT) and its variants to guide the search. However, in environments where rewards are sparse or deceptive, UCT's deterministic exploration bias can cause agents to get stuck in suboptimal regions of the joint action space.

2. Contribution

This paper provides the first simple regret analysis of Dec-MCTS in deceptive multi-agent trees. We then introduce CB-MCTS, a distributed algorithm that addresses these limitations. CB-MCTS replaces the deterministic UCT selection with a stochastic Boltzmann policy and incorporates a decaying entropy-based bonus to progressively focus on high-value actions. Coordination among agents is achieved through a marginal contribution function that aligns each agent's local decisions with the global objective.

While Boltzmann exploration has been applied in single-agent MCTS (Cesa-Bianchi et al. 2017; Painter et al. 2023), CB-MCTS is, to our knowledge, the first to adapt it to multi-agent planning. We show theoretically that CB-MCTS achieves exponentially faster decay of simple regret than D-UCT-based Dec-MCTS in deceptive trees.

Definition marginal contribution function
A coordination mechanism where each agent estimates how much its own action improves the global team reward, then picks the action that maximizes that contribution. Aligns decentralized policies with the joint objective.
