Question navigation inside a chat — 4 variants

Long branches make it hard to find an earlier question. All variants index the user messages of the current branch (questions are the natural anchors — every answer hangs below its question). Clicking a question scrolls it into view — without a flash (user correction 2026-07-22: the flash stays reserved for highlight jumps from the drawer; deliberate question navigation should be calm).

Decision (grill session 2026-07-22): variants 01 + 03 are FINAL and built together; 02 and 04 are rejected. Every user message counts as a question (no filtering). Scroll-spy derives the current question (last one whose top is above the viewport middle) — one source of truth for the stepper counter and the active popover entry. The header button appears from 2 questions; the stepper as soon as the list overflows, with the end arrows disabled. Alt+↑/↓ works globally but stays muted while an input field has focus; esc closes the popover. Popover entries strip the ask-in-chat quote prefix (quote-only messages show the quote in italics), reduce markdown to plain text and clamp to 2 lines; a click jumps (no flash) and closes the popover.

01Header button "Questions" with popover list — FINAL (built)

A small button in the chat header shows the question count. Clicking it opens a popover listing every question (2-line clamp), the one currently in view is highlighted. Click an entry → scroll + flash, popover closes. Zero space cost when closed.

Diffusion policies vs. ACT

How does the action chunking in ACT reduce compounding errors?
Action chunking predicts a sequence of k actions at once, so the policy commits to a short open-loop plan. Fewer decision points means fewer opportunities for small errors to accumulate, and the L1 loss on whole chunks encourages consistent trajectories…
And what exactly does the temporal ensembling do on top of that?
At every timestep the policy re-predicts an overlapping chunk. Temporal ensembling averages all still-valid predictions for the current step with exponential weights, which smooths the executed trajectory without adding latency…

03Floating prev/next stepper above the composer — FINAL (built)

A floating pill in the bottom-right corner of the message area: previous question, counter, next question.
