Context banner (Variant 3) — sub-variants & expanded state

Follow-up to mockup-context-pane-concept.html §03: the banner lives in the branch chat column ("context belongs to the chat that receives it"). Three sub-variants of how the expanded state opens. The expanded content uses the gist + key points presentation from mockup-context-pane-readability.html §02, with rendered math, a "show literal text" escape hatch, and a footer that repeats the contract.

01 3a — inline accordion

The banner expands in place and pushes the messages down. Zero new surfaces — but with 3–4 ancestors the accordion can swallow half the column, and the chat scroll position shifts.

02 3b — drawer over the chat column

Clicking the banner slides a drawer down over the messages, with a scrim behind it — the same pattern as the highlights drawer, so it costs no new UI vocabulary. The chat underneath keeps its scroll position; Esc or the X closes.

03 3c — chip row, one popover per ancestor

Instead of one banner, a slim "Knows about:" row with one chip per ancestor. Clicking a chip opens only that ancestor's summary as a popover. Most compact and most granular — but the "everything is sent together" contract is less visible than in 3a/3b.
