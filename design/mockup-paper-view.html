Diffusion Policy: Visuomotor Policy Learning via Action Diffusion

Cheng Chi · Siyuan Feng · Yilun Du · Zhenjia Xu · Eric Cousineau · Benjamin Burchfiel · Shuran Song · 2023

Abstract

This paper introduces Diffusion Policy, a new way of generating robot behaviour by representing a robot's visuomotor policy as a conditional denoising diffusion process. We benchmark Diffusion Policy across 15 different tasks from 4 benchmarks and find that it consistently outperforms existing state-of-the-art robot learning methods.

1. Introduction

Policy learning from demonstration, in its simplest form, can be formulated as the supervised regression task of learning to map observations to actions. However, the unique nature of predicting action chunks for physical robots presents challenges such as multimodal action distributions, sequential correlation, and the need for high precision — none of which are well handled by simple regression.

Diffusion Policy addresses these challenges by directly modeling the conditional distribution of action sequences using a conditional denoising diffusion process. The model is trained to denoise corrupted action sequences over T iterations, starting from pure noise.

2. Method

We use a U-Net architecture to predict the noise component at each denoising step. The model conditions on observation features extracted by a ResNet encoder. At inference time, we sample a random noise vector and iteratively denoise it using the learned model to produce a clean action sequence.
